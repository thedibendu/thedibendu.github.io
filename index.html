// About

Decoding the
machines of life.

I am a Ph.D. student in Marc C. Morais Group working on ATP-driven molecular motors involved in DNA translocation and viral genome packaging. My research combines protein biochemistry, cryo-electron microscopy, computational image analysis, and model building to understand how protein machines generate force and move DNA at the molecular scale.

I am especially interested in computational approaches for cryo-EM structure determination — including model fitting into electron density maps, workflow automation, and Python-based analysis.

// Research Interests

Where structure
meets function.

⬡

DNA Translocases

ATP-driven motors that actively move double-stranded DNA across membranes and through protein channels.

⬡

Molecular Motors

SpoIIIE/FtsK-family proteins coupling ATP hydrolysis to directional DNA transport during cell division.

⬡

Viral DNA Packaging

Bacteriophage packaging motors — among the most powerful force-generating machines in biology.

⬡

Cryo-Electron Microscopy

Single-particle analysis, data-processing pipelines, and high-resolution structure determination.

⬡

Model Building

Fitting atomic models into cryo-EM density maps and interpreting conformational states of complexes.

⬡

Computational Workflows

Python-based automation of cryo-EM pipelines, scripting for image analysis, and custom data tools.
